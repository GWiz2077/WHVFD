West Hartland Volunteer Fire Department Receives Funding from NCCF for New UTV to Enhance Firefighting and Rescue Capabilities

Date: August 5, 2025

The West Hartland Volunteer Fire Department is proud to announce the addition of a new Utility Task Vehicle (UTV) to its fleet, made possible through the generous support of the Northwest Connecticut Community Foundation (NCCF) Douglas and Janet Roberts Fund.

The new UTV will play a critical role in protecting the forests and residents of Hartland and surrounding communities. Outfitted with specialized equipment including a water tank, pump, and rescue basket, the vehicle is designed to fight brush and wildland fires deep within wooded areas, while also enabling firefighters and EMTs to quickly reach and transport injured patients from rugged terrain.

“This UTV gives us the ability to respond faster and more effectively to emergencies in areas that are difficult to access with larger fire apparatus,” said Matthew Wilson, Fire Chief of the West Hartland Volunteer Fire Department. “Thanks to a grant from the Northwest Connecticut Community Foundation Douglas and Janet Roberts Fund, we’ll be better equipped to protect both our community and the natural environment that surrounds it.”

The UTV will be fitted with a skid unit, which integrates both firefighting and rescue capabilities into one compact system. The addition of this equipment ensures that responders have the tools they need to suppress fast-moving brush fires and provide rapid emergency medical care in remote locations.

The West Hartland Volunteer Fire Department, staffed entirely by volunteers, provides fire suppression, emergency medical services, and rescue operations to the residents of West Hartland and surrounding communities. This latest addition underscores the department’s ongoing commitment to enhancing public safety and emergency response readiness.

The department extends its deepest gratitude to the Northwest Connecticut Community Foundation for making this purchase possible.
